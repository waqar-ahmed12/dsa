Our Algorithms

A variety of algos are used to solve any maze.

Optimization
A* Algorithm

The A* (A-star) algorithm is a widely used pathfinding and graph traversal algorithm in computer science and artificial intelligence. It is designed to efficiently find the shortest path from a starting point to a goal node, considering both the cost of reaching the goal and the heuristic estimation of the remaining distance.

Thoroughness
Depth First Search

DFS explores mazes by delving deeply into one path before backtracking, emphasizing thorough exploration over finding the shortest path. Its depth-focused strategy is well-suited for unraveling the intricate structure of a maze, making it suitable for applications prioritizing comprehensive exploration rather than optimizing for the quickest route.

Discovery
Breadth First Search

BFS systematically explores maze paths level by level from the entrance, making it ideal for finding the shortest path when all paths have equal weight. It ensures the discovery of the shortest solution by exploring paths based on their distance from the starting point.
